Preperation

When you are ready to purchase your Pygmy Marmoset Monkey, make sure you do some research first. You want to make sure you go to a reputable pet store or breader not a backyard breader. It is important that you know yor baby Marmy is in perfect health because they are not cheap. It is also important that the new addition to your familly has been hand fed and properly socialized several different people, so your Marmy doesn't have any issues aclimating to thier new suroundings or family members.

Supplies

If you take a look on the web, it is not hard to see that Marmoset Monkeys can cost any where from $1500 to $10,000. However, I must warn you that If you are purchasing your monkey from a reputable seller, the price will be on the higher end. If you choose to purchase a monkey from a seller on the lower end you may fall into the trap of a scammmer, or you may get a monkey that is sick or unsocialized. "

Training

It is highly important for you to begin training your monkey from the moment you bring them into your home, just as you would a new puppy. If you purchase your monkey from a reputable breeder some of this training may have been started for you. It is important to socialize your monkey as a baby so your monkey will get along with others in the household. You can train your monkey to do small tasks with positive reinforcement. If your monkey is socialized and comfortable with the trainer, it will be easier. As you can imagine, training a monkey is much like training any other animal and takes time and patience.

Lot's Of Love

Your new baby monkey will need lots of love. You should love your monkey just as you would your child or a new puppy as they will be a part of your family for a very long time. In a lot of ways, monkeys are like small children and they require a lot of attention from you. If you feel you do not have the time to dedicate to your tiny furry friend, maybe a monkey is not for you. However, if you know you have lots of love to give, you are patient and can dedicate a lot of time to your monkey, “Go for it!”.
